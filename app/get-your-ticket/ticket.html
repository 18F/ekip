About the Every Kid in a Park Annual Pass

  1. The Every Kid in a Park Annual Pass is FREE.
  2. The pass is only available to students in the 4th grade during the 2015-2016 school year.
  3. The pass may be obtained by exchanging the voucher in person at Federal entrance booths or gate houses that charge entrance or standard amenity fees. Find those sites here: https://store.usgs.gov/pass/PassIssuanceList.pdf
  4. If you are unable to access the sites listed above and exchange this voucher for a pass, the 4th grader (the pass owner) may continue to use this voucher. The voucher and pass are interchangeable.
  5. The pass covers entrance to Fish and Wildlife Service and National Park Service sites that charge Entrance Fees, and Standard Amenity Fees at Forest Service, Bureau of Land Management and Bureau of Reclamation sites. It will also be accepted at National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration and US Army Corps of Engineers sites.
  6. The pass admits the 4th grader (the pass owner) and any accompanying passengers in a private non-commercial vehicle at per vehicle fee areas, or the pass owner and up to additional 3 adults at sites that charge per person.
  7. The pass is non-transferable.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the benefits for the Every Kid in a Park Pass for 4th Graders?

  1. Free admission at sites that charge entrance or standard amenity fees for September 2015-August 2016.
  2. Allows pass owner and accompanying passengers in a single, private, non-commercial vehicle to enter federally operated recreation sites across the country.
  3. Covers the pass owner and three (3) accompanying adults age 16 and older at sites where per person entrance fees are charged. No entry fee charged for children 15 and under.
  4. The pass does not cover or provide a discount on expanded amenity fees such as camping, boat launch or interpretive fees.

What is not covered by the Pass?

The Pass does not cover Expanded Amenity fees such as camping, boat launching, parking, special tours, special permits or ferries. Also, some facilities and activities on Federal recreation lands (including those mentioned above) are managed by private concessionaires. The concessionaires charge for their services as any private company does and the Pass is not valid for their services.

Who qualifies for the Every Kid in a Park Pass for 4th Graders?

Only 4th graders during the 2015-2016 school year are eligible for the pass.

Where can I use my pass?

Please contact a site directly if you have a question about pass acceptance and fees. The Forest Service, the National Park Service, Fish and Wildlife Service, Bureau of Land Management, Bureau of Reclamation, US Army Corps of Engineers, and National Oceanic and Atmospheric Association honor the pass at sites where Entrance or Standard Amenity Fee(s) are charged.
